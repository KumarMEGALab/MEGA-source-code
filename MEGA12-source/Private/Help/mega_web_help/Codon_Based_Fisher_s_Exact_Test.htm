Codon Based Fisher's Exact Test

Distance | Codon A codon is triplet of nucleotides that codes for a specific amino acid. Based Fisher’s Exact Test

This provides a test of selection based on the comparison of the numbers of synonymousRH_Synonymous_change and nonsynonymousRH_Nonsynonymous_change substitutions between sequences.  Use this command to conduct a small sample test of positive selection At the DNA sequence level, positive selection refers to selection in favor of nonsynonymous substitutions. In this case, the evolutionary distance based on nonsynonymous substitutions is expected to be greater than synonymous substitutions. (Zhang et al. 1997Zhang_et_al_1997): a one-tailed Fisher’s Exact test.  If the resulting P -value is less than 0.05, then the null hypothesis of neutral evolution (strictly neutral and purifying selection Purifying selection refers to selection against nonsynonymous substitutions at the DNA level. In this case, the evolutionary distance based on synonymous substitutions is expected to be greater than the distance based on nonsynonymous substitutions.) is rejected.  If the observed number of synonymousRH_Synonymous_change differences per synonymous site (pS) exceeds the number of nonsynonymousRH_Nonsynonymous_change differences per nonsynonymous site (pN) then MEGA sets P  = 1 to indicate purifying selection Purifying selection refers to selection against nonsynonymous substitutions at the DNA level.
