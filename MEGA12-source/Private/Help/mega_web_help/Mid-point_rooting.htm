Mid-point rooting

In the mid-point rooting method, the root of an unrooted tree An unrooted tree is one in which no assumption is made regarding the ancestor of all the taxa in the tree. is placed at the mid-point of the longest distance between two taxa A taxon is the individual unit whose evolutionary relationship is being investigated. Depending on the study, “taxa” may refer to species, populations, individuals, or sequences within an individual. in a tree.
